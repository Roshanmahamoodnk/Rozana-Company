Market fit

Why this wins in Kerala

  • Culture: coconut is already part of daily food.
  • Trust: the ingredient is familiar and accepted.
  • Demand: households, hotels, and retailers already buy coconut products.
  • Tourism: premium tropical packaging works well for visitors.
Price architecture

Move up the value chain

  • Low value: raw nuts, unbranded fresh sales
  • Mid value: desiccated coconut, milk, paste
  • High value: VCO, shelf-stable beverages, premium snacks
  • Extra value: byproducts, coir, charcoal, carbon
Process

Factory flow designed for speed, hygiene, and scale.

1

Source

Aggregate mature and tender coconuts from farms and local traders with traceability.

2

Grade

Sort by maturity, size, freshness, kernel quality, and intended product line.

3

Process

Wash, split, extract, grate, press, dry, filter, or carbonize based on SKU.

4

Control

Track hygiene, moisture, shelf life, seal integrity, and sensory consistency.

5

Pack

Use bottles, pouches, aseptic cartons, jars, and industrial packs by channel.

6

Sell

Reach Kerala, metro India, hotels, export, and institutional buyers.

Roadmap

Build the award-ready brand in phases.

Phase 1

Start with VCO, coconut milk/paste, and one premium snack or beverage line. Validate demand and operational quality.

Phase 2

Phase 3

Expand into coir, charcoal, craft products, export packaging, and a strong B2B supply network.
